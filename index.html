Hello! I am Pengyu Liu (刘芃妤).

I am currently a PhD student in the Institute for Astronomy at the Univeristy of Edinburgh, under a joint program with the Leiden Observatory at Leiden University.

My research

I am working with Dr. Beth Biller and Dr. Matthew Kenworthy on the direct imaging and characterization of exoplanets and brown dwarfs. We use ground-based telescopes and space telescopes to search for exoplanets with novel detection tecniques and post-processing algorithms. I am interested in observations, data reduction, algorithm development and instruments (yes, everything) for direct imaging of exoplanets. One of my PhD projects is studying the variability and weather of young planetary-mass objects.

A little bit about myself

I come from Chongqing, a city in southwest of China.
